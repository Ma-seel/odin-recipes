Biryani Recipe

Description :

Experience the rich and aromatic flavors of this classic biryani recipe. This dish features layers of fragrant basmati rice and tender, marinated meat, slow-cooked with a blend of spices that infuse every grain with deep flavor. The biryani is enhanced with caramelized onions, fresh cilantro, and mint, creating a dish that's as visually stunning as it is delicious. A hint of saffron adds a luxurious touch, making this biryani a perfect centerpiece for any special occasion or family gathering.

Ingredients :

  • 1 lb (450g) chicken or lamb, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz/400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala or garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tbsp ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 tsp saffron threads (optional)
  • 2 cups water or chicken broth
  • Salt to taste

Steps you should follow

Preparation :

  1. Marinate the Meat: In a bowl, mix the chicken or lamb pieces with yogurt, biryani masala, turmeric, red chili powder, cumin, coriander, garlic, ginger, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for best results.
  2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  3. Fry the Onions: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown and crispy. Remove half for garnishing later.

Cook the Biryani:

  1. Cook the Meat: Add the marinated meat to the pot with the remaining onions. Cook over medium heat until the meat is browned and cooked through, about 10-15 minutes. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  2. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Stir in the diced tomatoes and cook until they break down and blend into the sauce. Adjust seasoning with additional salt if needed.
  3. Layer the Biryani: In a separate pot, bring water or chicken broth to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked (it should still have a bite). Drain the rice and layer it over the cooked meat mixture. Sprinkle with saffron threads, fresh cilantro, and mint. Cover tightly with a lid.
  4. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and cook the biryani for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook. Avoid lifting the lid frequently.

Serve:

    Once cooked, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Garnish with the reserved crispy onions and additional fresh cilantro and mint if desired. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.
